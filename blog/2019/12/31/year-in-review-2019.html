Year in review - 2019

blog

I have been deciding to write my "year in review" for about a month now. I thought I didn't have anything to share but I suppose I was being narrow minded. My life had changed drastically, and I have become a better well rounded person.

We moved to Norway. Moving continents without a lot of support was a lot of hard work. Post move, it was also difficult to adjust to a much lower temperature. Bangalore was 15-25. In Norway 15-18 is supposed to be a great summer temperature.

I got a job at a machine learning startup as a python system dev with some lightweight devops. It's just 10 minutes walk away from our apartment. I get to learn a lot about managing people. I personally think learning more tech won't have too many surprises. I ended this year becoming a software architect.

I learned to cook. I think I deserve a large round of claps for doing so. Well, it isnt much, but I can whip up a good meal given 3 hours. Not a magician, but quite good, even if I say so myself.

I have a very, very small garden. Its mostly herbs. But I do plan to be a lot more active from now. It uses grow lights and a timer. Just need to get the watering part automated.

Rupsa got me an activity tracker. It's something I didn't know I needed. Using this, I try to walk about 7500 steps a day and watch my diet. It has made my moods much better and I am much happier.

I am learning to drive. I understand it's meh for a lot of people, but I sure am loving it!

Now let's see if I can list out the things I want:

I want to read more, less netflix. More novels. More learning.

I want to get a driving license. Here in Norway it needs a lot of commitment and is more of a long term process.

I wish I could say more, but the only thing I am certain about life is how uncertain it is!
